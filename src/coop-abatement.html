Cooperative and Condominium Tax Abatement

This abatement can reduce the amount of property taxes owed by co-op and condo owners. Managing agents and boards must apply for this benefit on behalf of their entire property.

Apply or renew online

Eligbility

Applicants must meet all the requirements to be eligible for this abatement.

Requirements

  • The property must be classified as a tax class 2 property.
  • The co-op or condo unit must be the owner's primary residence.
  • The owner must have purchased the unit on or before January 5 to qualify for the abatement for the upcoming tax year.
  • New owners must have filed a Real Property Transfer Tax (RPTT) form or deed with the Division of Land Records at www.nyc.gov/acris.
  • Prevailing wage affidavits must be filed for certain properties. View a list of properties required to file the prevailing wage affidavit.

Restrictions

  • Unit owners cannot own more than three residential units in any one property. One of the units must be the owner’s primary residence.
  • Owners cannot also be receiving any of the following exemptions or abatements:
    • J-51 exemption
    • 420c, 421a, 421b, or 421g
    • Clergy property tax exemption
  • The property cannot be any of the following:
    • A Housing Development Fund Corporation (HDFC)
    • A Limited Dividend Housing Company
    • A Redevelopment Company
    • A Mitchell-Lama Building
    • In the Division of Alternative Management Programs (DAMP).
    • Part of the Urban Development Action Area Program (UDAAP)
  • The unit cannot be owned by a business (LLC).
  • The unit cannot be held by sponsors or their successors in interest.
  • Units owned by a trust are eligible only if the unit is the primary residence of all beneficiaries of the trust, trustee, or life estate holder.

Benefit

This abatement can reduce the amount of property tax you owe. The level of abatement is based on the average assessed value of residential units in the property.

Average assessed value Property tax reduction
$50,000 or less 28.1%
$50,001 - $55,000 25.2%
$55,001 - $60,000 22.5%
$60,001 and above 17.5%

Deadlines

The deadline to apply for or renew this benefit is February 15.

If February 15 falls on a weekend or holiday, the deadline is the following business day.

Apply, renew, or report changes

Managing agents and boards of directors can apply for this abatement online or by mail. Individual unit owners can’t apply for the abatement and must contact their managing agent or board to apply on their behalf.

Managing agents and boards are legally required to renew the abatement for all eligible units and notify the DOF of any changes in ownership or eligibility of units in their property by the deadline each year.

Frequently asked questions

What is the prevailing wage affidavit?

The prevailing wage affidavit certifies that all building service employees currently employed or to be employed at the property receive the applicable wage for the duration of the property’s tax abatement.

Failure to file the affidavit will result in the entire building being ineligible to receive the abatement. Prevailing wage is enforced by the City Comptroller. If you file an affidavit but are found to not have paid the proper prevailing wage, the building could be subject to civil penalties and may be ineligible to receive the abatement.

How do I know if my property is required to submit the prevailing wage affidavit?

Prevailing wage affidavits must be filed for properties that have

(1) 30 or more residential dwelling units and an average unit assessed value of more than $60,000, or (2) less than 30 residential dwelling units and an average unit assessed value of more than $100,000.

Check the list of properties to see if you’re required to submit the prevailing wage affidavit.

Please note that the list of properties will be updated in January, after the tentative property tax roll for the next tax year is released. The DOF will notify the managing agent or board if their required filer status changes due to a change to the property’s tentative roll assessed value (for example, as the result of a Tax Commission action).

Who must sign the prevailing wage affidavit?

The affidavit must be signed by an officer of the cooperative or the condominium or by an authorized agent. A managing agent may be an authorized agent provided the managing agent is authorized by the board to sign on behalf of the cooperative or the condominium.

Am I required to file the prevailing wage affidavit even if there are no building service workers in my property?

If your property is required to file the prevailing wage affidavit but has no building service workers, you must still complete, notarize, and submit the affidavit. Please check the box on the affidavit to indicate that your property does not employ building service employees.

How do I opt out of receiving the abatement?

If you choose not to provide the prevailing wage affidavit or don’t wish to continue receiving this abatement for another reason, you must opt out. To opt out of receiving the cooperative/condominium abatement, log in to www.nyc.gov/ccabatement and submit the “Co-op/Condo Tax Abatement Benefit Opt-Out” form.

Co-op/Condo Tax Abatement Benefit Opt-Out form

You only need to submit the opt-out form once. The abatement will be removed effective July 1 following the deadline to file. To apply for the cooperative/condominium abatement in future years, you must comply with all requirements and submit a new application.

If my commercial exemption is expiring, can I still apply?

If the property’s commercial exemption (such as a 421a or J51 exemption) is due to expire at the end of the tax year on June 30, you can still apply for the co-op/condo abatement. We will determine your eligibility to receive the abatement upon review of your application.

My property used to receive this abatement, but didn’t last year. Should I submit a new application or a renewal?

If your property lost the co-op/condo abatement in the previous tax year, you must file an initial application as a new applicant. You can only file a renewal if the property is currently receiving the abatement.

When will I start receiving my abatement?

Eligible properties with applications submitted or postmarked on or before the deadline will start receiving their abatement on July 1 of the same calendar year.

Eligible properties with applications submitted or postmarked after the deadline will start receiving their abatement on July 1 of the following calendar year.

What if my property doesn’t have a board, managing agent, or association?

If you live in a property that doesn’t have a managing agent, board, or association, contact the Department of Finance for assistance.
